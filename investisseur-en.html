Solidarity Financing

Investor Guide

Crowdfunding and community bonds

This guide details the terms allowing citizens to support the project cohabitat.cc through the acquisition of community bonds, in accordance with the laws governing solidarity financing in Quebec.

1. Legal Framework and AMF Exemptions

To solicit public savings without producing a full prospectus (a complex and costly document), an NPO like ours uses exemptions provided by the Securities Act (LVM).

  • Exemption for NPOs (Art. 3, par. 3 of the LVM) :

    This provision allows a non-profit organization to issue debt securities if no remuneration is paid for the sale of these securities. This is the main foundation of the community bond in Quebec.

  • Regulation 45-106 on prospectus exemptions :

    This regulation defines investor categories and allows raising funds from 'close relations' (friends, family, business partners) without strict amount limits, provided a real connection exists.

2. Investor Profile: Accredited vs Non-Accredited

The Autorité des marchés financiers (AMF) establishes strict criteria to protect savers based on their financial situation.

Criteria Non-Accredited Investor (General Public) Accredited Investor
Individual Income < 200 000 $ / year ≥ 200 000 $ / year (for 2 years)
Couple Income < 300 000 $ / year ≥ 300 000 $ / year (for 2 years)
Financial Assets < 1 000 000 $ (nets) ≥ 1 000 000 $ (excluding residence)
Net Assets < 5 000 000 $ ≥ 5 000 000 $ (all inclusive)

For cohabitat.cc: Even if you are a 'non-accredited' investor, you can invest significant amounts (e.g., $50,000 or $100,000) using the exemption for 'friends, family, and close business partners', because you are actively participating in the project's foundation.

3. Bond Characteristics

The community bond is a debt acknowledgment governed by the Civil Code of Quebec.

  • Nature of the security: An 'unsecured claim'. This means that in the event of bankruptcy, the investor is reimbursed after priority creditors (e.g., the bank holding the mortgage).
  • Interests: Interest paid is considered investment income by Revenu Québec and the CRA. It must be declared by the investor, unless converted into an official donation (if the NPO is a registered charity). Reference: Tax treatment of interest income - Revenu Québec

4. Risk Analysis (Mandatory Disclosure)

Any information document must include the following statements to comply with AMF expectations:

No Review

No securities regulatory authority has assessed the quality of these securities.

Risk of Loss

The investor could lose their entire capital if the real estate project does not generate the expected revenues for repayment.

No Secondary Market

These securities are not liquid. They cannot be resold on a stock exchange. Transfer to a third party often requires the issuer's approval.
